Chris O’Dowd Biography

Chris O’Dowd is an Irish award-winning and nominated Actor and Comedian who was born and brought-up in Boyle, County Roscommon. He is widely known for his role in the comedy film ‘Bridesmaids’ as Officer Rhodes, which earned him an IFTA Award for Best Supporting Actor, and three nominations of Screen Actors Guild Award, Broadcast Film Critics Association Award, and Phoenix Award for Best Ensemble Acting.

Chris is also known for his appearances and roles in the Channel 4 sitcom ‘The IT Crowd’ as Roy Trenneman, the comedy-drama series ‘Get Shorty’ as Miles Daly, and in the adult animated sitcom ‘Family Guy’ as the voice of Butler, Contestant, Guard. His role as Sean Murphy in the Irish sitcom ‘Moone Boy’, earned him two awards of International Emmy Award, and IFTA Award, and four nominations of Broadcasting Press Guild Award, and three IFTA Awards.

Chris O’Dowd Career

Chris O’Dowd Bridesmaids

Chris portrayed Officer Rhodes in the 2011 American comedy film “Bridesmaids”.  The film was directed by Paul Feig, written by Annie Mumolo and Kristen Wiig, and produced by Judd Apatow and Barry Mendel.  It was released on May 13, 2011 in the United States, and later dates to other countries.

His role earned him an IFTA Award for Best Supporting Actor, and three nominations of Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture, Broadcast Film Critics Association Award for Best Acting Ensemble, and Phoenix Award for Best Ensemble Acting.

Chris O’Dowd It Crowd

O’Dowd portrayed Roy Trenneman in the British sitcom series “The IT Crowd”. The series was created by Graham Linehan, edited by Paul Machliss, and executive produced by Ash Atalla. His role is that of the an IT technician from Ireland. He despises his job and often goes to great lengths to distract his workmates so he can do nothing. It was originally released on Channel 4 from 3 February 2006 to 27 September 2013.

Chris O’Dowd Get Shorty

Chris portrayed Miles Daly in the American comedy-drama television series “Get Shorty”. The series was created by Davey Holmes, produced by Sara E. White and Laura Jacqmin, and executive produced by Davey Holmes and Adam Arkin. It was aired on Epix from 13 August 2017 and 17 November 2019. His role is that of an enforcer for Amara De Escalones, a casino owner and gang boss in Pahrump, Nevada, who looks to escape his criminal lifestyle and enter into the film industry.
