Who Owns Liquid Death? Liquid Death Company History

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, August 3, 2024

Liquid Death is a popular canned water brand, known for its unique branding and marketing strategy. But who owns Liquid Death? Let’s take a closer look at the company’s history and ownership.

Liquid Death was founded by Mike Cessario, a graphic designer and marketing professional. Inspired by concertgoers drinking water out of Monster Energy cans at a music festival, Cessario saw an opportunity to create a water brand with unconventional branding. In 2019, Liquid Death was officially introduced to the market.

The brand quickly gained recognition for its edgy tagline, “Murder Your Thirst,” and its distinctive packaging. The water is meticulously sourced and canned to perfection. As of 2023, the water is now canned by Wilderness Asset Holdings LLC in Virginia, USA.

Despite being a relatively young company, Liquid Death has already made significant waves in the beverage industry. The brand offers a range of products, including sparkling water and flavored carbonated beverages, to cater to various consumer preferences.

Over the years, Liquid Death has received substantial investments that have contributed to its growth and success. Science Inc., a prominent venture capital firm, and Live Nation Entertainment, a leading entertainment company, have both shown confidence in the brand. This has helped Liquid Death achieve a valuation of $700 million as of October 2022.

With a commitment to sustainability, Liquid Death promotes the use of aluminum cans to reduce plastic waste. The brand also donates a portion of its proceeds to initiatives aimed at combating plastic pollution, reflecting its dedication to making a positive impact.

The Origins of Liquid Death

Liquid Death, a unique and unconventional bottled water brand, was founded by Mike Cessario, a graphic designer and marketing professional. The company’s journey began in 2009 when Cessario noticed concertgoers hydrating from Monster Energy cans at a music festival. This observation sparked his creativity and led him to envision a brand that would challenge the norms of the bottled water industry and engage consumers in a distinct way.

Driven by his passion for innovation and humor, Cessario capitalized on this opportunity and embarked on the creation of Liquid Death. Finally, in 2019, the brand officially made its debut after securing seed funding from Science Inc., a prominent venture capital firm. Since then, Liquid Death has experienced tremendous success, captivating a wide audience and disrupting the market with its audacious concept and unparalleled marketing strategy.

Liquid Death Products

Liquid Death offers a variety of refreshing and distinctive products to quench your thirst. Whether you prefer still water, sparkling water, or flavored carbonated beverages, Liquid Death has something for everyone. The brand’s commitment to quality and taste is evident in every sip.

One of the most iconic features of Liquid Death is its packaging. The water comes in 16.9 US fl oz (500 ml) “tallboy” cans, which have become synonymous with the brand. The sleek and eye-catching design grabs attention and sets Liquid Death apart from traditional bottled water brands.

The original water was sourced from the pristine Austrian Alps, ensuring its purity and exceptional quality. It was then skillfully canned by an Austrian beverage company, maintaining the integrity of the water from source to packaging.

As Liquid Death evolved, it expanded its product line to include a range of flavored carbonated beverages. These bold and exciting flavors include Mango Chainsaw, Severed Lime, Convicted Melon, and Berry It Alive. Each flavor is carefully crafted to deliver a unique and memorable taste experience. Liquid Death also offers tea flavors such as Grim Leafer, Rest in Peach, and Dead Billionaire, catering to those seeking a refreshing tea alternative.

The Success of Liquid Death

Liquid Death, since its launch, has been enjoying tremendous success in the bottled water market. The brand’s unique marketing approach and distinctive branding have struck a chord with consumers, particularly among millennials and Gen Z. This has fueled the company’s rapid growth and popularity.

In 2021 alone, Liquid Death generated an impressive revenue of $45 million, showcasing its undeniable market appeal and generating substantial profits. This revenue milestone is a testament to the brand’s ability to captivate consumers and convert their preferences into sales.

Furthermore, the success of Liquid Death has caught the attention of prominent investors, such as Science Inc. and Live Nation Entertainment. These investors recognize the brand’s potential and have made significant investments to fuel its growth and expansion. This support from established industry players speaks volumes about the brand’s credibility and future prospects.

As a result of its remarkable achievements, Liquid Death has reached a valuation of $700 million as of October 2022. This valuation reflects the brand’s strong market position and its ability to differentiate itself from traditional bottled water offerings.

Liquid Death and Sustainability

Liquid Death, the popular canned water brand, is committed to promoting sustainability and making a positive impact on the environment. As part of their efforts, the brand focuses on using aluminum cans for packaging, which are more environmentally friendly than plastic bottles. By choosing aluminum, Liquid Death helps reduce plastic waste and supports the recycling industry.

Furthermore, Liquid Death has taken additional steps to address plastic pollution. The company has pledged to donate a percentage of its proceeds to support initiatives dedicated to reducing plastic waste. Their donations have already exceeded $1 million, demonstrating their commitment to making a meaningful difference in environmental conservation.

By championing sustainability, Liquid Death aligns with the values of eco-conscious consumers who seek brands that prioritize environmental responsibility. Their dedication to sustainable practices resonates with individuals who strive to make more environmentally conscious choices.
