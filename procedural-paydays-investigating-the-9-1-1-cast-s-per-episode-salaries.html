  • 9-1-1 cast members Oliver Stark, Ryan Guzman, Aisha Hinds, and Kenneth Choi each make $100,000 per episode.

Lights, sirens, and big paychecks – that is the world of 9-1-1, the procedural action drama that has been on air since its explosive premiere in 2018.

Brought to life by the powerhouse team of Ryan Murphy, Brad Falchuk, and Tim Minear, 9-1-1 chronicles the lives of Los Angeles first responders, from police officers and firefighters to dispatchers, as they bravely face life-or-death situations on a daily basis.

The show's success stems from its high-octane action sequences, riveting storylines, and a talented ensemble cast. 9-1-1 features veteran actors like Angela Bassett and Peter Krause alongside rising stars like Oliver Stark and Ryan Guzman.

Audiences have praised the cast for their dynamic portrayals of police officers, firefighters, paramedics, and emergency call dispatchers.

9-1-1 has consistently drawn in millions of viewers each week, with the sixth season maintaining a steady viewership of around four million people per week.

Given its status as one of the most acclaimed dramas on prime-time television, it is natural to wonder how well the talented cast is compensated.

From the esteemed Angela Bassett to the fresh faces keeping us on the edge of our seats, let's find out the salaries of the 9-1-1 cast.

7 Oliver Stark

Salary Per Episode - $100,000

Oliver Stark brings life to the impulsive and endearing Evan 'Buck' Buckley, a young firefighter who is a staple of the 9-1-1 crew.

Buck's growth is a core element of the show. He navigates the often perilous world of a first responder, while evolving from a reckless risk-taker to a more mature and responsible individual.

Fans have followed his journey since the show's beginning, witnessing his professional achievements and romantic entanglements.

Stark's salary has seen a steady increase over the season. In season five, he received a significant raise, with Deadline Hollywood reporting a 25 percent increase in his paycheck.

Stark currently earns $100,000 per episode on 9-1-1. With season six consisting of 18 episodes, Stark's earnings for the season could reach $1.8 million.

Stark's estimated net worth is a comfortable $2 million.

6 Ryan Guzman

Salary Per Episode - $100,000

Ryan Guzman joined 9-1-1 in season two, taking on the role of Eddie Diaz, a firefighter and a single father.

Eddie's storyline centers on finding the delicate balance between the demands of his job and raising his young son, Christopher, who has cerebral palsy.

Despite joining the cast later in 9-1-1's run, Guzman earned $100,000 per episode for season six, on par with several of his co-stars.

According to reports, Guzman's salary was increased for season five, going from $80,000 to $100,000 per episode, following a reported 25 percent raise.

This consistent paycheck adds to his growing net worth, approximated to be around $2 million.

5 Aisha Hinds

Salary Per Episode - $100,000

Aisha Hinds brings intensity and heart to the role of Henrietta Wilson, a skilled paramedic and firefighter.

Hinds, a veteran actress recognized for her work on True Blood and Underground, anchors the show with her grounded presence.

Hen's journey is as compelling as any emergency: navigating a medical career, family life, and her own sexuality. Hinds delivers this complexity with nuance, making Hen a fan favorite.

In addition to her role in 9-1-1, Hinds has also appeared in the spin-off series, 9-1-1 Lone Star, reprising her role. From season six, Hinds was paid $100,000 per episode on the show.

With 18 episodes in season six, Hinds's total earnings likely reached $1.8 million. By the end of season seven, which contains ten episodes, she will take home at least $1 million.

Aisha Hinds has an estimated net worth of $3 million.

2 Peter Krause

Salary Per Episode - $300,000

Peter Krause, a seasoned actor known for his roles on Six Feet Under and Parenthood, anchors the 9-1-1 cast as Robert "Bobby" Nash, as the stoic and experienced captain of LAFD Station 118.

Krause has been a cornerstone of the show since its inception, guiding his team through thrilling rescues and intense personal dramas.

1 Angela Bassett

Salary Per Episode - $450,000

Angela Bassett plays LAPD patrol sergeant Athena Grant-Nash, a determined leader in the show.

Through her multi-layered performance, Bassett has anchored the show since its inception, earning her numerous accolades as well as a historic paycheck.

Bassett's $450,000 per episode salary positions her as the highest-paid cast member on the show, and one of the highest-paid actresses of color in network drama series history.

Ahead of season five, Bassett signed a lucrative new deal along with other series regulars, making her the top earner on the show.

The deal saw Bassett pocket $16.2 million for the sixth and seventh seasons.

Bassett also serves as an executive producer for both 9-1-1 and its spin-off, 9-1-1: Lone Star.

Angela Bassett has an estimated net worth of $25 million.
