Taylor Swift's Era's Tour Pictures Are Giving Us Summer Music Festival Vibes

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, August 13, 2024

Taylor Swift has posted some of her concert/ Era's tour pictures and besides being jealous, they are making us anxious for this summer's music festivals! It's concert season! Summer is all about music festivals! Of course, we already had Coachella, but there is so much more going on than just that. Below, we have a collection of other must-haves for whatever type of music fest you are attending this summer!

Maybe this is your first year going to a music festival. You are in the right place! You may not have known there was even such a thing as festival wear, but there is, and it looks a little different for different music genres, but one thing remains the same: music is a language everyone speaks, and when people from all aspects of life can come together and share a common bond, that's pretty neat.

It can be challenging to choose the ideal outfits to wear to a music festival. Wearing something fashionable and festive will highlight your individual style to its fullest. The physical activity that music festivals necessitate, however, requires that you also make sure you'll be comfortable enough for it. Walking around the festival grounds and dancing to your favorite musicians will keep you mostly on your feet. You must be prepared for the heat and sunshine because it will be hot, and what if it rains?

What Shoes Should You Wear To A Music Festival?

When dressing for a music festival, comfort must come first. This does not prevent you from dressing stylishly and expressively. Simply put, it means that you must consider the performance of the clothing you choose to wear. Are the style points really worth it if your shoes are excruciatingly painful?

Older sneakers are preferable to new ones. They don't have to be completely beaten up, but regardless of what you choose, you must be prepared for them to witness some abuse. That requires you to trade in your designer sneakers for something you don't mind getting stepped on and dirty.

Related:Taylor Swift Jumped On Board The Kelly Green Trend And Nailed It

Because they are fashionable and durable, boots are a good choice. Just make sure you've broken them in and are certain that your feet won't be in pain after wearing them for a few hours. Also, think about the heat before going with a leather thigh high or something similar. A practical way to guarantee that your shoes will be kind to your feet throughout the festival is to use a comfortable insole. Just be sure to test them out first because insoles occasionally cause friction between your foot and the shoe that wasn't there before, especially if they're thicker than the ones you replace.

What Shoes Not To Wear To A Music Festival

They may be cozy in other circumstances, but even if you can stand all day in them, your feet will almost certainly be filthy by the time the day is over. Dust, grime, and other particles will coat your toes like crazy. Not to mention the possibility of having your toes trampled in a crowded area.

This one should be self-explanatory by this point. Even though heels might be the perfect finishing touch for your ensemble, they're not worth it. Take a few pictures wearing your heels if you must, then go out wearing something more appropriate.

Protecting Yourself From The Sun At A Music Festival

Sunglasses are an accessory that will give your outfit a flashy appearance. They are also necessary for prolonged sun exposure. A music festival allows you to wear any type of sunglasses you like, but it's the ideal occasion to don a flashy pair that you usually leave at home.

Put on a hat to shield your face from the sun, whether it's a cowboy hat, bucket hat, brim hat, or something fun and quirky like a baseball cap. Although the sun is our best friend, it is also out to get us. The use of hats is essential for sun protection.

A bandana is a trendy, multipurpose accessory that goes well with the Bohemian style. Wear it over your face to shield it from dust and germs; wear it around your head to keep sweat and hair out of your face; wear it to block the sun from your neck.
