How Much Do Target Employees Make? Salary and Hourly Wages

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, July 29, 2024

Target Corporation, one of the largest retail chains in the United States, offers competitive pay and benefits to its employees. If you’re curious to know how much Target employees make, including their salary and hourly wages, you’ve come to the right place. In this article, we’ll explore the salary range, benefits, and overall compensation package that Target provides to its dedicated team members.

Target believes in the importance of fair compensation and recognizes the critical role its team members play in the success of the company. As a result, the company has recently raised its starting wage range for hourly employees. The new starting wage ranges from $15 to $24 per hour, depending on the job and local market. This update positions Target as a leader in the retail industry when it comes to employee wages.

In addition to competitive hourly rates, Target also offers a comprehensive benefits package to its team members. This package includes healthcare benefits, virtual physical therapy, enhanced fertility benefits, and faster access to 401(k) plans. Target’s commitment to its employees’ well-being and overall success sets it apart from other employers in the retail industry.

As an employee at Target, you not only receive a competitive salary, but you also have the opportunity to grow your career. With industry-leading investments in its team members, Target provides a range of benefits and opportunities that contribute to a rewarding and fulfilling work experience.

Key Takeaways:

  • Target’s starting wage range for hourly employees is from $15 to $24 per hour.
  • Target offers a comprehensive benefits package, including healthcare benefits, virtual physical therapy, enhanced fertility benefits, and faster access to 401(k) plans.
  • Target’s investments in its team members have helped create rewarding careers and opportunities for growth.
  • Target’s average hourly rate is $16.46, with wages ranging from $13.47 to $23.61 per hour depending on the position.
  • Compared to other retailers, Target’s compensation package and commitment to employee well-being make it an attractive employer.

New Starting Wage Range at Target

Target Corporation recently made an announcement regarding a new starting wage range for its hourly team members. Moving forward, employees can expect a competitive salary ranging from $15 to $24 per hour. This change reflects Target’s commitment to rewarding its employees for their hard work and dedication.

The exact starting wage within this range will be determined based on the specific job and the local market. Target recognizes the importance of ensuring fair compensation that aligns with the demands of each role and the cost of living in different areas.

In an effort to invest in its workforce, Target is allocating up to $300 million to raise its minimum wage. This significant investment demonstrates the company’s commitment to improving the financial well-being of its employees and ensuring they are better off for choosing to work at Target.

With this wage increase, Target is positioning itself as a leader in the retail industry when it comes to employee compensation. By offering a competitive starting wage range, Target aims to attract and retain top talent, creating a motivated and dedicated workforce.

Expanded Access to Health Care Benefits

Starting in April, Target is enhancing its commitment to the well-being of its hourly team members by offering expanded access to comprehensive health care benefits. In the past, team members had to work at least 30 hours per week to be eligible for Target’s medical plan. However, with the introduction of new changes, team members who work a minimum average of 25 hours per week will now be eligible for these benefits.

This significant expansion will make health care benefits available to approximately 20% of Target’s team members who previously did not qualify. By lowering the average weekly hour requirement, Target aims to prioritize the health and well-being of its employees, ensuring that a greater number of team members have access to affordable and comprehensive medical coverage.

The company’s decision to increase eligibility for health care benefits is a testament to its commitment to its employees’ overall compensation package and their overall well-being. Target recognizes the importance of ensuring that its team members have access to the necessary medical resources to support them in both their personal and professional lives.

Investments in Target Team Members

Target Corporation is committed to supporting the well-being and success of its team members through various investments. Over the past five years, Target has implemented a range of programs and benefits designed to enhance employee satisfaction, professional development, and work-life balance.

This initiative enables team members to pursue higher education or improve their skills without the burden of student loans. In addition to educational support, Target prioritizes the health and well-being of its team members. Target also understands the importance of stable schedules for its team members. In an effort to provide more stability and predictability, the company has implemented initiatives to ensure schedules are consistent and predictable, allowing employees to better plan their personal lives around their work commitments.

Furthermore, in response to the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, Target has provided additional benefits to support its team members. These benefits include free backup care, paid leaves, and support for COVID-19 vaccines. Target’s commitment to the health and safety of its employees during these challenging times reflects its dedication to employee well-being.
