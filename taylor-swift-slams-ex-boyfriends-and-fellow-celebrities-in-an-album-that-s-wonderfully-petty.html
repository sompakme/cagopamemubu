Taylor Swift Slams Ex-Boyfriends And Fellow Celebrities In An Album That's Wonderfully Petty

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, September 4, 2024

Taylor Swift absolutely shocked her fans with the release of a secret 'side B' to her 11th studio album, "The Tortured Poets Department". In total, she released 31 new songs. There's no doubt that her music has resonated with millions and millions of fans worldwide. So much so that they endlessly dig into the true meaning of her most popular songs. And usually, these lyrics reference one of her many famous ex-boyfriends, such as Joe Alwyn.

In her latest album, Swift absolutely rips apart numerous ex-boyfriends as well as other celebrities. This article will delve into which songs were about Matt Healy and Joe Swlyn. Then we will discuss the Jake Gyllenhaal and John Mayer references in "Tortured Poets Department". Finally, we will touch upon what Swift said about the Kardashian/Jenner family in her new work.

"The Tortured Poets Department" Has Songs About Taylor Swift's Relationship With Joe Alwyn And Matt Healy

What surprised Taylor Swift fans was the inclusion of songs seemingly about her current boyfriend, Travis Kelce. What didn't surprise fans was the sheer amount of songs allegedly about her ex-boyfriend of six years, Joe Alwyn, and her controversial situationship with 1975 frontman, Matt Healy.

While the big hit single from "Tortured Poets Department", "Fortnight", is certainly about an ex-boyfriend, it is inconclusive as to whether it's about Alwyn or Healy. The titular track on the album, however, is almost definite about Healy. The reference in the lyrics to a tattooed smoker is kind of a dead giveaway, according to Vulture. There's no doubt that Matt Healy is the 'tortured poet' of which Swift speaks.

"My Boy Only Breaks His Favorite Toys" is certainly about Alwyn but also has a hint of Alwyn, according to Vulture. Same with "Down Bad". Then there's "So Long London", which is about the breakup between her and Joe Alwyn.

"He will not be surprised by the song. Him and her know what went on."

Is There A Song About John Mayer On Taylor Swift's "Tortured Poets Department"?

According to Just Jared, Swifties are convinced that Taylor Swift referenced her extremely brief and turbulent relationship with John Mayer in her new album, "The Tortured Poets Department". Specifically, they believe that "The Manuscript" is about her short time with Mayer. This is due to the mentioning of an age gap between the two of them

  • John Mayer is 12 years older than Taylor Swift
  • Taylor Swift and John Mayer were allegedly romantically linked in 2009

The lyrics to "The Manuscript" that fans believe alludes to John Mayor are as follows:

“In the age of him, she wished she was thirty / And made coffee every morning in a French press / Afterwards she only ate kids’ cereal / And couldn’t sleep unless it was in her mother’s bed.”

In "The Manuscript", Swift also writes, "She thought about how he said since she was so wise beyond her years / Everything had been above board."

  • "The Manuscript" is the 31st track on "The Tortured Poets Department"

While Swift hasn't confirmed whether or not the son is about him, she has allegedly written about Mayer before. This would be on her "Midnights" album. Specifically, the song, "Would've, Could've, Should've".

Among the 15 bonus tracks dropped hours after the initial release of "The Tortured Poets Department", is "The Manuscript". While some Swifites believe the song is about John Mayer, due to the age gap between him and Taylor Swift, others believe it's actually about Jake Gyllenhaal.

What Did Taylor Swift Write About Kim Kardashian In "The Tortured Poets Department"?

While Taylor Swift exposed a lot about herself in her 11th studio album, "The Tortured Poets Department", including how she is a "functioning alcoholic", fans are sure she took numerous shots at a ton of famous people. While her ex-boyfriends were clearly the subject of many of the album songs, a lot of Swifties believe Swift took a shot at Kim Kardashian, reigniting their feud.

According to The Daily Mail, Taylor Swift's "thanK you alMee" is about the Skims mogul and reality star. Part of this is due to the fact that "KIM' is spelled out in the oddly undercapitalized song title. The song, of course, isn't just about Kardashian as her ex-husband Kanye West also factors into the equation. The song discusses a feud Swift had with a girl named "Aimee" from her hometown.

Another lyric lends more proof that the song could be about Kim Kardashian. That being, “One day, your kid comes home singin’ a song that only us two is gonna know is about you."

According to TheTab, this is allegedly referencing the TikTok that Kardashian's daughter North West posted singing a Swift song.

"The Tortured Poets Department" also features a song called "Cassandra" which could also be about Kim Kardashian. Or, at the very least, may reference her. Fans believe the lyrics refer to an edited phone call Kardashian and Kanye shared with Swift, attempting to lessen the song feud they once had.

“When the first stone’s thrown, they’re screamin’ / In the streets, there’s a raging riot / When it’s “Burn the b-tch,” they’re shrieking / When the truth comes out, it’s quiet."

The song also references snakes, which is an emoji Kardashian once used on Instagram to describe Taylor Swift, according got Just Jared. On top of this, Swift appears to take a shot at the entire Kardashian/Jenner family as a whole, describing them as "pure greed".

"Cassandra" may or may not be about Kim Kardashian and Kanye West. But fans sure think it is. But that's the beauty of Taylor Swift's music; she always leaves her songs up for interpretation.
