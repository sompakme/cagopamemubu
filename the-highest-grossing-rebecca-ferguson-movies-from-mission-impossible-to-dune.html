Highlights

  • Rebecca Ferguson has starred in blockbuster hits like Mission: Impossible and Dune, grossing $4.1 billion globally.
  • Ferguson's role as Miranda North in Life contributed to its $100.5 million box office success.
  • The highest-grossing Rebecca Ferguson movie to date is Mission Impossible: Fallout.

Rebecca Ferguson's time in Tinseltown has been a whirlwind of critical acclaim and blockbuster dominance.

She began her acting career with the Swedish soap opera Nya Tider, and went on to make her film debut in the slasher film Drowning Ghost (2004).

However, her international breakthrough came in 2013, with her Golden Globe-nominated performance as Elizabeth Woodville in The White Queen.

Since then, she's become a fixture in action and sci-fi franchises, starring in over 20 films that have amassed $4.1 billion at the global box office.

In roles ranging from the steely agent Ilsa Faust in the Mission: Impossible franchise to Lady Jessica Atreides in the epic Dune, she has both critical acclaim and commercial appeal.

Her standout performances include her portrayal of Jenny Lind in the beloved musical film The Greatest Showman, and Miranda North in Life. She had supporting parts in the science fiction films Dune and Dune: Part Two.

Rebecca Ferguson's net worth is estimated at around $6 million, and today, we'll explore the numbers behind her impressive filmography, ranking her highest-grossing films.

10 Life (2017)

Global Box Office, Life - $100,541,806

While not a runaway blockbuster, Life holds a unique place in Rebecca Ferguson's resume and the sci-fi horror genre.

Directed by Daniel Espinosa, the film stars an ensemble cast consisting of Jake Gyllenhaal, Ryan Reynolds, and Ferguson herself.

Ferguson takes on the role of Miranda North, a calm and collected quarantine enforcement officer onboard the International Space Station.

The crew makes a groundbreaking discovery â€“ the first signs of extraterrestrial life on Mars. However, their excitement quickly turns to terror as the alien lifeform rapidly evolves, exhibiting surprising intelligence and deadliness.

Despite comparisons to classics like Alien, Life mainly garnered positive reviews for its acting, suspenseful story, and intriguing visuals.

The film performed modestly at the box office, grossing $30.2 million domestically and $70.3 million internationally for a global total of $100.5 million.

This number was a solid return on its estimated $58 million production budget.

9 The Girl On The Train (2016)

Global Box Office, The Girl On The Train - $173,185,859

Rebecca Ferguson departs from her typical action heroine roles in this twisty psychological thriller to play Anna, a seemingly glamorous and put-together woman entangled in a missing person investigation.

Based on the bestselling novel by Paula Hawkins, the film follows the perspectives of three deeply flawed women, headlined by Emily Blunt.

Ferguson's performance brings an enigmatic quality to Anna, making audiences question whether she's a victim, a suspect, or something far more insidious.

The Girl On The Train proved to be a box office smash, raking in $75.4 million at the domestic box office and $97.8 million overseas, bringing its worldwide total to $173.2 million.

With a production budget of just $45 million, the movie was a major financial success.

7 Hercules (2014)

Global Box Office, Hercules - $244,819,862

Before her laudable performances in space operas and high-octane spy thrillers, Rebecca Ferguson left her mark on the sword-and-sandal genre with Hercules.

The film stars Dwayne Johnson as the legendary demigod, but Ferguson steals the show as Ergenia, a fierce warrior princess of Thrace.

The film follows Hercules, a self-proclaimed demigod, and his band of mercenaries as they lead the Thracian army against a warlord.

It received mixed reviews from critics, with some praise for Johnson's performance and the action sequences, though the screenplay and plot received criticism.

Hercules grossed $72.7 million in the United States and Canada and $172.1 million in other territories, for a total of $244.8 million worldwide.

6 Men In Black: International (2019)

5 The Greatest Showman (2017)

Global Box Office, The Greatest Showman - $438,550,306

The Greatest Showman, a 2017 biographical musical drama, dazzled audiences with its vibrant visuals, catchy songs, and star-studded cast.

Rebecca Ferguson joined the ranks as Jenny Lind, a renowned Swedish opera singer. Loosely based on the life of P.T. Barnum, the film delves into his journey of creating the famed Barnum & Bailey Circus.

Despite historical inaccuracies, The Greatest Showman proved to be a box office smash with a global total of $438.6 million.

According to Box Office Mojo, this includes a domestic take of $174.3 million and an international haul of $260.7 million, all against a production budget of $84 million.

3 Mission: Impossible - Dead Reckoning Part One (2023)

Global Box Office, Dead Reckoning Part One - $567,535,383

Rebecca Ferguson reprises her role as Ilsa Faust in the action-packed sequel, Mission: Impossible - Dead Reckoning Part One.
