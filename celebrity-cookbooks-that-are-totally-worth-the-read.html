Celebrity Cookbooks That Are Totally Worth The Read

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Monday, September 23, 2024

There is a reason why cooking shows have been on television for as long as television existed. In the simplest of terms, everybody eats! And if people dive deeper into food cultures, habits, and various diets, there is always a sort of celebration at the heart of good recipe which people can pass down across generations.

Knowing how to cook is just an overall very convenient and rewarding skill. And coming from all kinds of different cultures, all while being exposed to many more diverse eating habits and dishes, celebrities do take pride in being able to come up with their own creations, or recommend them for those who want to try something new.

The amount of celebrities who have cookbooks, or are passionate about cooking might be surprising to many fans, but there are indeed so many out there! And yes, sometimes these cookbooks might run the risk of being too detached from reality, i.e a normal household does not have the pantry that the Kardashians have. But other cookbooks contain plenty of recipes that are not only delicious and give fans an insight to what their favorite celebrities love to eat, but are also full of heart.

8 Back to the Kitchen By Freddie Prinze Jr. Should Get All Fans Of Great Cooking Back To The Kitchen

Aside from being the son of adored comedian Freddie Prinze, fans know and love Freddie Prinze Jr. from his days as a full time 90s and 2000s heartthrob. The actor shot to fame after I Know What You Did Last Summer, She's All That, and the Scooby-Doo films. He's also been married to the brilliant actress Sarah Michelle Geller since 2002 and the happy couple share two kids together.

Out of the actor's endless career achievements, the one that caught fans out of left field is the launch of his quite elaborate cookbook. The actor has successfully published Back to the Kitchen: 75 Delicious, Real Recipes (& True Stories) from a Food-Obsessed Actor, co-authored by Rachel Wharton, and it has a forward written by the actor's wife, Sarah Michelle Gellar. Turns out, Prinze Jr. is a skilled home cook, and learned it all from the life lessons his mother gave him, which always happened near a stove.

7 Chrissy Teigen Has All Her Fans' Cravings Covered

Chrissy Teigen is a famed model, and her career as an established fashionista is what allowed her to make a name for herself in the modeling and entertainment world. Her marriage to the world-famous singer John Legend did not distract her from her own endeavors and aspirations though. The model released not one, but two cookbooks, each showcasing her superior home cooking skills and immaculate palate. Her books became kitchen essentials worldwide when she released Cravings: Recipes for All the Food You Want to Eat in 2016 and Cravings: Hungry for More in 2018.

6 Reese Witherspoon's Whiskey In A Teacup Is Home To Some Fine Alabama Recipes

Fans need no introduction to the acting and production powerhouse that is Reese Witherspoon. She has been prolific for decades and gave fans their favorite cult classic films like Legally Blonde, Just like Heaven, and critical hits like Wild. For all her admirers and fans, Reese Witherspoon's book Whiskey In A Teacup is not just chock-full of recipes from the actress' home state of Alabama, it offers its readers deep insight into what living in the south has taught Witherspoon. The name of the book itself is inspired by what her grandma called southern women, that they're exactly like "whiskey in a teacup" in their strength and beauty.

5 Trejo's Tacos: Recipes and Stories from L.A. By Danny Trejo Is A Journey Across Memories And Flavors

Danny Trejo put his signature villain mask to the side as he prepared for writing a cook book with countless finger licking recipes. Trejo has honed his culinary skills into being a bona fide chef and a restaurateur and opened his first restaurant back in 2016 called Trejo's Tacos. His book is a treat for all lovers of authentic Mexican cooking, with a badass twist.

4 Tastes Like Schitt: The Unofficial Cookbook of Schitt's Creek Is A Collective Culinary Endeavor

No one could have ever guessed that a television series would have its own cookbook, that was until Schitt's Creek treated all its fans to one hell of a gem. Though the show wrapped up in 2020, it had numerous memorable food moments that fans were just happy to see collected in one book, and it's definitely a collector's item for fans of the show, along with the Schitt's Creek special edition Monopoly.

3 The Full Plate By Ayesha Curry Is The Real Chef In Her Household

The Full Plate by Ayesha Curry is as good as it gets. Curry entered the culinary game back in 2016 with the release of her cook book, The Seasoned Life: Food, Family, Faith, and the Joy of Eating Well which made her a New York Times bestseller. Her newest addition to her career as a chef was 2020's The Full Plate: Flavor-Filled, Easy Recipes for Families with No Time and a Lot to Do, and she definitely knows about that since she is a proud mother of three and navigates her career as a full time chef.

2 The Tucci Table By Stanley Tucci And Felicity Blunt Is A Cozy Couple's Cookbook

As if Stanley Tucci couldn't get more wholesome, he co-authored a full on cookbook with his wife Felicity Blunt back in 2014. In an interview, he expressed where his love for cooking began saying, "After I made Big Night, I learned quite a bit during the making of the film and writing the film, I kind of thought my love affair with food would end, but it didn't. It only got greater."

1 From Crook To Cook Is Snoop Dogg's Gift To The Culinary World

Many fans can remember the odd combination that was the team up between Snoop Dogg and Martha Stewart on her cooking show, some even thought they might be dating. But whatever their relationship was, Snoop Dogg must have picked up a thing or two about cooking from the iconic home cooking expert herself as he released his book co-authored by Stewart and Ryan Ford back in 2018 titled From Crook to Cook: Platinum Recipes from Tha Boss Dogg's Kitchen. The book actually got amazing reviews for its funny, simple and quite tasty recipes.
