Emma Watson's Film Career And Salaries

Emma Watson reached global prominence as a child with her debut professional acting role as Hermione Granger in the Harry Potter film series.

Before this, she had only performed in school plays. At the young age of ten, Watson was cast in Harry Potter And The Sorcerer's Stone, earning less than $1 million for her role.

Specific earnings for each Harry Potter movie remain undisclosed; it was reported that Watson received $4 million for her role in Harry Potter And The Order Of The Phoenix.

As the series progressed, Watson's salary was continuously increased, reaching $30 million for the two-part finale, Deathly Hallows (Parts 1 and 2). According to TheThings, Watson earned at least $70 million from the series.

While specific figures for her post-Harry Potter projects remain elusive, industry experts estimate she makes anywhere between $5 million and $10 million per film.

Beyond the wizarding world, Watson continued to make a mark, notably starring in the live-action remake of Beauty And The Beast, which grossed $1.26 billion.

Emma took home $15 million for Beauty And The Beast, with a base salary of $3 million and an additional $12 million in back-end bonuses based on the film's box office success.

Although Watson has appeared in more than 20 films, the box office success of the Harry Potter franchise contributed significantly to her net worth.

Watson has been ranked among the world's highest-paid actresses.

In 2017, Forbes recognized Emma Watson's financial achievements by ranking her number six (tied with Charlize Theron) on its list of the highest-earning actresses in film.

This recognition acknowledged her pre-tax earnings of $14 million, earned between June 2016 and June 2017.

In 2009, 19-year-old Emma Watson collaborated with the posh British brand Burberry. Her endorsement featured iconic items like beige trenches and tartan-patterned bags, adding a touch of glamour to the label.

Watson reportedly earned a six-figure sum for the campaign.

Watson also surprised many by endorsing Chanel in 2008. She promoted the scent Coco Mademoiselle, receiving two years of free fragrance as a portion of her compensation.

In June 2020, Watson was appointed to and became the youngest member of the board of directors of Kering, owner of various fashion brands (such as Gucci and Yves Saint Laurent).

Emma Watson's Real Estate

While details about Emma Watson's property portfolio remain largely private, available glimpses reveal a combination of smart investments and personal havens.

In 2008, at the age of 18, she purchased a luxurious ski chalet in Meribel, France, reportedly for around $1.8 million.

This charming retreat nestled in the French Alps has reportedly appreciated in value, estimated to be worth around $4 million.

Watson owns a home in London's affluent Hampstead neighborhood. Purchased in 2012 for a reported $3.3 million, the acquisition was executed through the offshore entity Falling Leaves in the British Virgin Islands.

Spanning over 2,200 square feet, the property features four bedrooms, two bathrooms, and two living rooms in a vintage, terraced style.

Additionally, Watson was rumored to have acquired a property in Los Angeles with her then-boyfriend, Leo Robinton, although concrete evidence of the sale remains elusive.
